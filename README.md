# Bellabeat Smart Device Usage Analysis
## Google Data Analytics Capstone Project

## Project Overview
Analysis of smart device usage data to identify activity trends 
and provide data driven marketing recommendations for Bellabeat, 
a wellness technology company targeting women.

## Tools Used
- Google Sheets — data cleaning, calculated columns, pivot tables
- Tableau — data visualization and dashboard

## Data Source
Fitbit Fitness Tracker Data via Kaggle — 33 users tracked over 
March to April 2016

## Key Findings
- Wednesday had the highest average daily steps at 7,511
- Tuesday had the lowest average daily steps at 4,915
- No day of the week met the WHO recommended 10,000 daily steps
- 67% of all tracked days fell into the Low activity category
- Activity levels increased in April suggesting seasonal influence

## Business Questions

### 1. What are trends in smart device usage?
Analysis of 457 tracked days across 33 users revealed consistent patterns in smart device usage and physical activity. Wednesday showed the highest average daily steps at 7,511 followed by Monday at 7,119 and Saturday at 7,090. Tuesday recorded the lowest average steps at 4,915 with Sunday being the second lowest at 6,058 — a significant gap of over 1,100 steps between the most and least active days. Notably no single day of the week met the WHO recommended 10,000 daily steps. Additionally the data which was collected between March and April 2016 showed increasing activity levels during April suggesting seasonal influence on physical activity with warmer months potentially driving higher engagement."

### 2. How could these trends apply to Bellabeat customers?
The trends identified in smart device usage can be directly applied to Bellabeat's customer base. Since Wednesday and Monday show peak activity levels Bellabeat can strategically time their marketing campaigns and app notifications on these days when customers are most engaged with their fitness. Additionally since 67% of tracked days fell into the Low activity category and no day met the WHO recommended 10,000 steps Bellabeat has a significant opportunity to differentiate their product by incorporating daily step goal reminders personalized activity targets and low activity alerts. This positions Bellabeat not just as a tracking device but as an active wellness coach helping women bridge the gap between their current activity levels and recommended health goals

### 3. How could these trends help influence Bellabeats marketing strategy?
The data findings present several clear opportunities to influence Bellabeat's marketing strategy. First Bellabeat should strategically launch marketing campaigns in March as users begin increasing their activity heading into warmer months capitalizing on natural seasonal motivation. Peak engagement days of Wednesday and Monday represent optimal timing for targeted digital advertisements and app based promotions when users are most active and receptive to wellness messaging.
Second Bellabeat should target individuals who are attempting to become more active but struggling to meet recommended goals — a segment clearly represented by the 67% of days falling into the Low activity category. This audience is already aware of their fitness through tracking but needs motivation and accountability to bridge the gap.
Finally Bellabeat should highlight product features that directly address these gaps including personalized daily step goals, low activity reminders, and achievement animations for calorie burning milestones. Positioning these features prominently in marketing materials transforms Bellabeat from a passive tracking device into an active wellness partner — a compelling differentiation in the competitive smart device market
